1.ultraportable

Ultraportable are thin and light laptops designed for mobility. This type of laptops cuts out some features for mobility such as CD/DVD drive and connection ports. Ultraportable laptops, sometimes called notebook PCs or just notebooks, are lightweight and thin computers. They typically have a 12-inch screen and run on lithium ion batteries for up to around three hours before needing recharging.

2.Ultrabook

Ultrabook is a term made by Intel for a special type of ultraportable notebook. A laptop is called an ultrabook when it meets certain criteria such as size/weight, battery life, type of chipset. While ultrabooks are typically more expensive than other laptops, they offer many features that make them worth the purchase price.
Laptops in this category can often last up to 20 hours on one charge, which is double or triple most full-size notebooks. They also have screens that are very easy to see outdoors because of their matte finish, high contrast ratio and lack of glare; some models also have touchscreens for even easier navigation through files on a computer without having to use an external keyboard or mouse.
